The graph illustrates the situation with lack of workplaces in different parts of the world since 2014 until 2015.
To begin with, North Africa represented stability. That is to say, the unemployment rate reached 12,5% during both 2014 and 2015. The same dynamics is showed in South Africa and Worldwide with 3,9% and 5,9%, accordingly. Middle East improved the indicators and declined unemployment by 1,4%. So do Latin America decreasing by 1%. On the contrary, the rate in EU had increase of 0,4%.
The worst indicators belonged to North Africa, while the best took place in South Asia. Besides, the latter one’s rate consisted a quarter of the first one’s. The median value related to EU. And the general rate of the whole world was double less, than the highest unemployment rate in the graph.
To sum it up, the common tendency in the world has changed slightly. Mostly, countries have stayed at the same level. Middle East and Latin America improved the situation, but only the latter part of the world has a leap in the world unemployment rating. It has moved from the fourth place to the fifth place.
