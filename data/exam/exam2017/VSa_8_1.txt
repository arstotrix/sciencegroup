The two pie charts illustrate the percentage of different kinds of goods transported by rail and road in Eastern Europe in the year 2008.
First of all, it is obvious from the diagram that metals are prefered to be transported by rail while food products prevail in transportation by road. More than that, one of the most noticible features of the pie charts is that the percentage of manufactured goods is almost equal and the difference make up only 3%. Moreover, the percentage of machinery transported by rail exceed sixfold the same figure of the second chart. Besides, the third goods transported by road is chemicals.
