The given charts demonstrates how the use of Facebook changed from 2012 to 2013 and how the reasons of using it differ between men and women.
The first chart shows the number of users who used two vesions of the site, desktop and mobile, in March 2012 and March 2013. In 2012 the desktop version of Facebook was used by 150 million people during the month. Their number decreased to below 140 million in March of 2013. The difference in the number of the mobile version users is more significant: it increased from less than 60 million a month in 2012 to almost 100 million users a month in the following year.
The second chart provides information on the main reasons men and women give when asked why they use Facebook. Sharing photos and videos is the most popular reason among women, 54% mentioning it as one of the main reasons, while less men think it is one. Recieving updates through Facebook is equally important for men and women, it was chosen as one of the main reasons by 39% of both men and women. Another popular reason of using Facebook is seeing funny posts: 43% of women think it is one of the main, although only 35% of men think the same. Just as many women mention of learning about ways to help others as of their main reason of Facebook use, while the percentage of men to say so it 10 percent less. Overall the given reasons for using Facebook are more relevant to women than to men.
