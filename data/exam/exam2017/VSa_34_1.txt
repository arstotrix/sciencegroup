The pie charts illustrates goods transported by rail and road in Eastern Europe. This charts have both similarities and differences.
As it can be seen from the charts machinery and metal goods are primary transported by trains and reach 12% and 35% comparing with 2% and 11% by cars. What both charts have in common is that the the percentage of manufactured goods and chemicals are almost equil in both kinds of transport, reaching 26% and 13% in average. It should be noted that food products and non-metallic minerals are prefered to be transported by road as car transportation takes less time.
Overall, the railway transportation is suitable for metals and machinery, while car transportation is better for food products, non-metallic minerals and manufactured goods.
