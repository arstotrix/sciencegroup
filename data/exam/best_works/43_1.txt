The bar-chart illustrates quantity of children who had not access to studying at primary school in Africa, South Asia and rest of the world in comparison between 2000 and 2012.

Overview, more children had got access to primary education in all region by 2012. The most dramatic change was in south Asia where number of children without access to primary schools dropped from 32,7 millions to 9,9 millions and reached the lowest number. The highest point was almost 45 million of children in Africa in 2000.

As for gender, boys and girls consisted almost equal parts in all regions at any time except the situation in South Asia in 2000 when number of girls was twice as number of boys.

Overall, the situation in the world had improved from 2000 to 2012 because of reducing number of uneducated children, especially more girls got access to primary school education.
