The given bar chart displays percentage of children, separately boys and girls, who had no access to primary education from 2000 to 2012 in Africa, South Asia and the Rest of the World.
As can be seen from the chart, the amount of boys and girls in Africa in 2000 we almost the same and were equal to approximately 20 percent.
Convercely, in south Asia in 2000 percentage of girls who had no access to primary education was twice as much as percentage for boys, and stood for 21.6 percent. Moreover, it dramatically changed by 2012 3 times devided from 30 percent overall to 10 percent. The same trend was true for Rest of World from 2000 to 2012 and dropped from 12.8 for girls in 2000 to 7.6 percent in 2012.
To conclude, it would be better to say that for all parts of the world access to primary education for children became more likely possible in 12-year period.
