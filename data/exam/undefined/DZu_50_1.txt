The graph illustrates changes in the number of children, compared in millions, which had no opportunity to attend primary school during thirteen-years period from 2000 to 2012. The diagram clearly highlight the fact, that overall number of kids without access to primary education has dropped by the end of the period.
The largest number of children, which remained uneducated, live in African countries accounted for 20 millions for boys and 23,7 for girls. Only a slight fall can be observed by the year 2012. At the same time, South Asian states managed to deal with the problem of low-educated population, because the number of children with no primary school knowledge decreased dramatically from nearly 35 millions to just 10 millions. In other countries around the world changes were not significant. Most children there have an access to primary school. What is more, this number enhanced on 15 millions.
