The provided chart illustrates changes in the number of children with no access to primary school education. The access of boys and girls from different countries are reported in a period from 2000 to 2012.
The first thing should be noticed is that from 2 provided groups (by gender) boys had access to primary education more than girls: 88,6 millions to 68,9 millions. It also seems a tendency for number of children to have more oportunities to get primary education since 2000 – on the example of South Asia – 32,7 millions of children with no access to education in 2000 and 9,9 millions in the end of current period. Therefore South Asia showed rapid development of education policy in a current period. Countries provided by name of “Rest of World” can be described as countries with low level of children without access to primary education in the beginning of a period.
