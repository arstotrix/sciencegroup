The given chart provides us information about amount of children who didn’t have access to primary education in the period from 2000 to 2012 by gender and region.
The main feature is that in all of the regions the number of kids without access to primary school declined. Another point is that during the whole period Africa remained that region, where is the highest rate of youngsters with no access to school. It also should be mentioned that in 2000 in Africa, South Asia and in the rest of the world there were more girls without opportunity to enter primary school than boys in 2012. This tendence remained the same only in Africa, where there were 18.2 millions of girls against 14.5 millions of boys.
All in all, summarizing the whole information, it must be said that by the end of the period amount of kids, who didn’t enter primary school became less in all regions of the world.
