The graph represents dynamics in the amount of children without ability to get primary school education during the period from 2000 to 2012 with arrangement by gender and region. First of all, Africa has the largest number of children without access to primary school education in the beginning and in the end of time, which was taken for the chart. In 2012 such amount of boys and girls in Africa is almost fivefold larger than in South Asia and roughly twofold bigger than in the Rest of World. Moreover, these watchings take place with the reduction of number of children with disability to get main school education. Secondly, South Asia has the lowest amount of  children with this problem in the end of period, comparising 4,8 millons to 7,6 in the Rest of World and to 18,2 in Africa among girls. The same dymanic may be analyzed about boys in these regions.
