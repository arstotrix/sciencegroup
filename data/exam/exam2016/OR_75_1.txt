The chart demonstrates the number of children who had no possibility to go to primary school from 2000 to 2012 in Africa, South Asia and rest of world. First of all, it is necessary to compare figures in one region, but in different years. By and large, the number of children without primary education decreased from 44 million in 2000 to 33 million in 2012. The same figure of 33 million of children had South Asia in 2000. This situation significantly changed in this region in 2012. The number of children without possibility to go to primary school reached a low of 10 million among Africa and rest of the world in any year. It is noticeable, that the level of children who had no possibility to go to school in rest of the world in 2000 and 2012 year is smaller, than in Africa in the same year and in South Asia in 2000.
