The graph compares data about kids who had no opportunity of study at primary school by males, females and regions during the period of 12 years. It is clear, that in all countries except South Asia the number of girls was higher than boys. It is noticeable, that in Africa there was about 45 million kids without access to primary school in 2000 and it drop up to 33 million in 2012. At the same time in rest of world there was a decline by million. In South Asia was also a sharp reduction from 33 million up to 10 million. There is a greater proportion of males and females in Africa in comparison with other regions. There was twofold decline in South Asia. Overall, by 2012 the amount of such kind of children decreased in all countries.
