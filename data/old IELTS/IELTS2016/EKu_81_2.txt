 &nbsp; Large, transnational corporations are becoming increasingly powerful. And that is why they think about moving their business to other countries. It is named offshoring. Offshoring is the relocation of a business from one country to another—typically an operational process, such as manufacturing, or supporting processes, such as accounting. Typically this refers to a company, although state governments may also employ offshoring. More recently, offshoring has been associated primarily with the outsourcing of technical and administrative services.
