The bar chart presents data relating to the number of children who have no access to primary school education during the period from 2000 to 2012. They are divided by two categories: gender and region.
It can be observed that the number of children who have no access to primary education was the most significant in Africa in 2000 (more than 40 millions of children). Nowadays this country still remains its position with approximately 35 millions of children. The same number had South Asia in 2000. However, it can be also revealed that it was a significant cleoline in this region from 30 millions in 2000 to 10 millions in 2012. As for the rest of world, it should be also noticed that it has the same tendency. By the way, as a rule, the number of girls who have no access to education are more than boys.
