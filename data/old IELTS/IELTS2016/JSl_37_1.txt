The graph provides the information about the quantity of children who could not afford to go to primary school in 2000 and 2012 in different regions devided by gengers. It can be seen that the trend of changes was downward in all regions. The most number of children without access to primary school education was in Africa in 2000. Despite the fact it had decreased for about 10 million people by 2012 Africa had stayed the leader. There was the sharp fall in the amount of children which did not have a possibility to study at primary school in South Asia from 2000 to 2012. It had declined for about 30 per cent and 20 of them is reducing among girls. All in all, the number of girl and boys who did not go to primary school had equally fallen from 2000 to 2012 all over the world.
