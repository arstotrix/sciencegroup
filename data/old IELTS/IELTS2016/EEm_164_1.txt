The data gives information about youngsters without opportunities to go to primary school from 2000 to 2012 by sex and geographic location.
The main trend is that number of children without access to primary school education has decreased in all regions. However, Africa had the biggest number in 2000 and 2012.
Firstly, South Asia had the lowest values by both genders in 2012 and their biggest falls in comparison with year 2000. Secondly, only in the rest of world and in South Asia in 2012 girls were able to go to primary school in bigger amount than boys.
