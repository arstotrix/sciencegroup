The bar chart illustrates the quantity of children of both genders that lack access to primary education in 2000 and 2012 in Africa, South Asia and in the rest of the world. Amount of children without access to primary education in Africa in 2000 was an upward trend with as many as 23.7 millions of girls and 20 millions of boys. However, this figure underwent changes and it decreased in 2012 to the same numbers that were in South Asia in 2000. In the rest of the world there was the smallest number of children without primary education in 2000: 12.8 and 10.5 millions of girls and boys respectively. Nevertheless, the situation shifted because in 2012 it was South Asia that reached a bottom of 10 millions children: 4.8 millions of girls and 5.1 millions of boys. Overall, there was a tendency of falling of the number of children without access to primary school education if we consider information given for all the regions and genders.
