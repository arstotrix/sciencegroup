The bar chart reveals gender contribution of various post-school qualifications in Australia in 1999. 
It is evident that men were more likely to study that year, as for five given qualifications men outnumbered women in three of them.
The biggest gap between numbers of men and women for one kind of diploma was for Skilled vocational diploma, where males got 90% of the whole amount. The percentage of females who got undergraduate diploma was twofold of percentage of males. The smallest gap between genders numbers was 10% for bachelor’s degree, when women overweighe men. Postgraduate diplomas were more popular amoung males as their numbers were more than twice bigger then for females. Master’s degree was more widespread among men, about 60% got it.
Overall it can be clearly seen that in 1999 the number of educated men was definetely higher than for women.
