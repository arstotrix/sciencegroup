The graph provides information about the proportion of the population aged 65 and over in the period from 1940 to 2040 in Japan, Sweden and the USA.
It can be said, that the proportion of population aged over 65 in Japan decreased gradually from 5% in 1940 to 4% in 1960 and remained stable during the period from 1960 to 1980, when it started to increase and reached 6% in the year 2010. According to the graph, this rise could continue and due to the sharp increase in the year 2030 it could reach 28% in the year 2040.
Similarly, the number of people aged 65 and over in proportion to the whole population in the USA rose constantly since the year 1960, when it leveled off at 14% until the year 2014, when it could continue to rise by 10%.
In Sweden the proportion of population aged 65 and over increased gradually throughout the period 1940-2014 and could continue to rise, reaching 26% in the year 2014.
Overall, it can be seen, that the USA and Sweden demonstrated a steady upward trend, while in Japan the proportion of population aged 65 and over decreased constantly until the year 1990.
