The bar chart illustrates the percentage of different post-school qualifications in Australia in 1999 and the gender proportion who held them. It can be clearly seen that skilled vocational diploma was the most men's popular qualification and at the same time a few number of women held it. Women was prefered undergraduate diploma. 
Skilled vocational diploma was chosen by about 90 percent of male, other 10 percent - by females. Undergraduate diploma was chosen by females as twice as many times it was chosen by males - about 68 percents versus 34. Bachelor's degree was the same level in both genders. Males prefered postgraduate diploma and master's degree much more than females. 
It is clear that women held undergraduate diploma and bachelor's degree, and men prefered skilled vocational diploma, postgraduate diploma and master's degree.
