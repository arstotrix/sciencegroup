The bar graph describes the amount of students, who came from abroad to finish their education in various Canadian provinces between 2001 and 2006. 
It is clear to see that in 7 of 8 universities the percentage of graduates increased in approximately 2 times during the period under the view. In the 8th University, namely Alberta, there was a downward trend in the amount of students. This educational institution took the last position in given universities in the province in 2006.
Conversely, British Columbia University had an increase in the flow of international graduates in approximately 2 times, rose from 4th place on 7th and became at the same level with Nova Scotia University by 2006. Two universities, Manitoba and Newfoundland & Labrador, had approximately the same percentage of students during the whole period under the consideration. 
The university New Brunswick held its position and in 2006 it was the educational institution with the highest amount of students from abroad like it was in 2001.
