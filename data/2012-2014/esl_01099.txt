The given diagram shows us the typical stages of consumer goods manufacturing, including the process by which information is fed back to earlier stages to enable adjustment. Firstly, we see the big system of manufacture, it something like semicircle. We can observe correlation between manufacture and product research. Also, producing moments depend on organizational moments. For example, production planning depends on design and packaging depends on market research. Information from product research important for design and information from market research important for packaging, advertising and sales. Storage and production planning have feedback with each other. 
   According to the information from the diagram, consumer goods manufacturing is very elaborate process, which include many actions and steps. Step from raw materials to packaging, which include storage, production planning, assembly, inspection and testing. There are also parallel process with it, like product research, design, market research and advertising. And in the end of process, dispatch and sales. Also important parts of manufacturing.
