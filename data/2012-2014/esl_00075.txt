Psychological  perspectives  on  entrepreneurship:  
cognitive and  social factors  in entrepreneurs'  success.
This article reveals issues such as why are  some entrepreneurs  so much  more  successful than  others  in starting new companies-ones that  create wealth  for  their  societies  as well  as  themselves? Relying on the results of the previous research about entrepreneurial success  is influenced in equal measure the social and cognitive factors. Based on studies by various authors it has been found that successful entrepreneurs appear to think differently than other persons in several respects and they appear to be higher in social competence. Additionally, the results suggest that  the principles and findings of psychology can  be  invaluable for providing important  insights into  the  factors  that  influence  entrepreneurs'  success.
