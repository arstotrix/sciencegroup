The line graph displays the quantity of people from different parts of the world who travelled to another countries from 1990 to 2008.
Overall, the number of people travelling internationally increased with the flow of the time. Comparing numbers of people from  all the parts of the world, it can be seen that the relation between them remained the same: the tourists travelled the most and the least from the same parts of the world both in 1990 and 2005.
North America is the only part of the world where there can be seen a downward trend from 2000 to 2005, while in all the other parts the numbers had been growing all the time. In Central and Eastern Europe there had been the substantial increase and the amount of international tourists had tripled. In South America and in South Africa the numbers had grown the least, from less than 10 millions to about 20 millions.
