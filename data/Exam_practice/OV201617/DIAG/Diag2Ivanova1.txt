The given graph depicts information about international tourist arrivals in period between 1990 and 2005. As it can be seen from the graph, the main trend was going upwards. Overtime the amount of tourists in these parts of the world have increased. 

The highest amount of tourists had always been in North America, and the lowest level had been in South America. The trends of Sub-Saharan Africa and South America are quite similar to one another, but the amount of tourists in Sub-Saharan Africa grew a little more than in South America, starting from 1995. It is also given, that tourists became interested in South-East Asia and Central and Eastern Europe. The rise in amount of tourists in Europe is the most rapid. Overall, the most popular region to travel to had always been North America. From 1990 to 2000 the interest in this part of the world had been only risen. Despite that from 2000 to 2005 there was a slighly fall in the amount of visitors, North America remained the most interesting country for travellers. It is important to say that by the year 2005 the amount of visitors in North America and Europe became almost the same.
