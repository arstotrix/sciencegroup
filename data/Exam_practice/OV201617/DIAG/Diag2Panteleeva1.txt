The graph illustrates the relation among the numbers of journeys in different places of the world from 1990 to 2005. Overall, the number of people visiting North America during this period is the highest indicator, as the line of this country is higher than others.
To begin, all indicators, that show how many arrivals were hold, have risen since 1990. The least number of visitors was in South America in 1990. The lines of South America and Sub-Saharan Africa are practically similar. It means that the number of people who visited these countries from 1990 to 2005 was nearly the same. In 2005 the number of visitors arrived in Central and Eastern Europe and North America reached 90 percent. By the way, from 2000 to 2005 the number of people visiting North America did not practically change.
All in all, the more the time passed, the more people visited North America, Central and Eastern Europe, South-East Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa and South America.
