Tongue-twisters are short, but not simple texts for the learning, filled with difficult consonants and sound combinations. Tongue-twisters help to develop the speech apparatus in order that in the future the child will have an expressive, clear and legible speech. Besides, tongue-twisters are almost always perceived aurally and the result of pronunciation will depend on how you hear, remember and understand such short challenging texts. So tongue-twisters promote the development of not only the articulation of a child, but also of the memory and speed of thinking and information assimilation. Moreover, studying tongue-twisters is simply a funny and captivating occupation for the child.
