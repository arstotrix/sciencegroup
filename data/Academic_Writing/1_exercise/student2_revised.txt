Including tongue-twisters in language programs of primary school may result in forming a fluent speech and increase it's pace which will be very helpful in the future.
Tongue-twisters are used to develop a correct pronounciation and sometimes for entertainment purposes.
That's because they contain words with several identical sounds, which make it hard to pronounce fast and without mistakes.
They are engaging and can stimulate pupil's ambitions to do it correctly.
Practice is very important , so after some exercises, like repeating a tongue-twister several times, a person will be able to pronounce even an unknown sentence without making mistakes.
As a conclusion, the government should consider including tongue-twisters in a school program.
