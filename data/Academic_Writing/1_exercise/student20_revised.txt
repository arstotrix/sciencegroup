Tongue-twisters are mostly associated with fun and laughter, as these tricky phrases or poems with alliteration are designed in the way it is very easy to slip accidentally while pronouncing. However tongue-twisters are not only for linguistic fun and games. From the very childhood it is useful for a child to develop his or her speech, to enhance its fluency and pronunciation of individual words. This is the main reason for the tongue-twisters to be included into the language programs of primary schools. With the help of everyday training child’s speech will soon become correct, audible and clear. Moreover, children try to penetrate into the meaning of words and phrases more sensible during the process of memorization without any hurry. That’s why children learn to pay attention to details. To conclude, tongue-twisters are very effective not only for improving the child’s concentration but also for his or her entertainment in the process of studying.
