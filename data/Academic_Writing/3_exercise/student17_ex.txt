1) The authors expressed in their article good and relevant ideas, though they are not probative and exhaustive.
2) Despite the fact that the authors provided a lot of bright and persuasive examples, generalization about how we perceive the world cannot be made on their basis.
3) The authors would sound even more convincing if they provided a wider range of examples connected with gender marking. 
4) The convincing and a bit romantic conclusion of the article helps to summarize all the arguments stated before.
5) Guy Deutscher poses a good and interesting hypothesis, though his argumentation could be more concrete.
6) The discussion would have been more relevant if all the participants had contributed their ideas to it.
7) The article would have benefited from introducing the data from typologically rare languages.
